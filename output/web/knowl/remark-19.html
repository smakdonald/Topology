Remark 5.4.17.
There are 3 views of G × H: Using presentations (if G = \langle A | R \rangle and H = \langle B | S \rangle, then G × H = \langle A ∪ B | R ∪ S ∪ {ab = ba | a ∈ A, b ∈ B} >), as a Cartesian product set with componentwise multiplication, and as the largest group generated by G and H such that the subgroups G and H commute.
