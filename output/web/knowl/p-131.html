A basis on a set \(X\) is a collection \(ℬ\) of subsets of \(X\) satisfying:
  1. For each \(p\) in \(X\) there is an \(N_p\) in \(ℬ\) such that\(p ∈ N_p\text{,}\) and
  2. whenever \(N_1,N_2\) are in \(ℬ\) and \(p\) is an element of \(N1 ∩ N2\text{,}\) then there is an \(N_3\) in \(ℬ\) such that \(p ∈ N_3 ⊆ N_1 ∩ N_2\text{.}\)
