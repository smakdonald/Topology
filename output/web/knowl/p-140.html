  1. Let \(c\) be a real number. The function \(f:\R \to \R \) defined by \(f(r) := cr\) for all \(r \in \R \) is continuous.
  2. The functions from \(\R^2\) to \(\R \) defined by addition, subtraction, and multiplication are continuous.
  3. The function \(g:\R \times (\R \setminus \{0\}) \to \R \) defined by \(g(x,y) := x/y\) is continuous.
  4. A uniform limit of continuous functions from \(\R \) to \(\R \) is continuous; hence sine and cosine are continuous.
