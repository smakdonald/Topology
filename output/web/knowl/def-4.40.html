Definition 4.3.8.

Let X and Y be topological spaces, let Z be a subspace of X, and let g,h: X \to Y continuous functions that satisfy g|Z = h|Z. A homotopy from g to h relative to Z is a family of maps ft: X \to Y for all t \in I such that
  1. f0 = g,
  2. f1 = h,
  3. the function H: X \times I \to Y defined by H(p,t) := ft(p), for all p \in X and t \in I, is continuous, and
  4. ft|Z = g|Z for all t \in I.
The function H is also called a homotopy from g to h relative to Z. The function g is said to be homotopic to h relative to Z, written g \simeq Z h.
