Let \(p: (X̃,x̃_0) \to (X,x_0)\) be a covering space, let \(Y\) be a PC and LPC space, and let \(f: (Y,y_0) \to (X,x_0)\) be continuous.
  1. If \(f∗(\pi_1(Y,y_0)) \sse p∗(\pi_1(X̃,x̃_0))\text{,}\) then there is a unique continuous function \(f̃: (Y,y_0) \to (X̃,x̃_0)\) such that \(p \circ f̃ = f\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f∗(\pi_1(Y,y_0)) ⊈ p∗(\pi_1(X̃,x̃_0))\text{,}\) then there does not exist a continuous lift \(f̃: (Y,y_0) \to (X̃,x̃_0)\) such that \(p \circ f̃ = f\text{.}\)
