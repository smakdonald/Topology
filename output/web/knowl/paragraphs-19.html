Definition 7.2.33.
Let X be a topological space with subspaces A,B such that X = Int(A) \cup Int(B). The chain complex C•(A+B) is defined by: Cn(A+B) := {∑ m\sigma \sigma + ∑ kττ | each \sigma ,τ is a singular n-simplex of X, each \sigma (\delta n) \sse A and τ(\delta n) \sse B, and each m\sigma , kτ \in \Z } and ∂nA+B := ∂nX|Cn(A+B)Cn-1(A+B).
Proposition 7.2.34.
Let X be a topological space with subspaces A,B such that X = Int(A) \cup Int(B). The sequence 0• \to C•(A \cap B) \to C•(A) ⊕ C•(B) \to C•(A+B) \to 0•, with homomorphisms φ̃n: Cn(A \cap B) \to Cn(A) ⊕ Cn(B) given by φ̃n := (iAB n♯,-iBA n♯) and ψ̃n: Cn(A) ⊕ Cn(B) \to Cn(A+B) given by ψ̃n := jA n♯ + jB n♯, is exact.
Theorem 7.2.35. Small Chains Theorem.
Let X be a topological space with subspaces A,B such that X = Int(A) \cup Int(B). Then Hn(X) \cong Hn(A+B) for all n.
