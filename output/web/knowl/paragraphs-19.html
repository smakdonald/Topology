Proof of the MV Theorem.

Definition 7.2.33.
Let \(X\) be a topological space with subspaces \(A,B\) such that \(X = Int(A) \cup Int(B)\text{.}\) The chain complex \(C•(A+B)\) is defined by: \(Cn(A+B) := {∑ m\sigma \sigma + ∑ kττ | \text{ each }\sigma ,τ \text{ is a singular} n-\text{ simplex of }X, \text{ each }\sigma (\delta n) \sse A \text{ and }τ(\delta n) \sse B, \text{ and each }m\sigma , kτ \in \Z }\) and \(∂nA+B := ∂nX|Cn(A+B)Cn-1(A+B)\text{.}\)
Proposition 7.2.34.
Let \(X\) be a topological space with subspaces \(A,B\) such that \(X = Int(A) \cup Int(B)\text{.}\) The sequence \(0• \to C•(A \cap B) \to C•(A) ⊕ C•(B) \to C•(A+B) \to 0•\text{,}\) with homomorphisms \(φ̃n: Cn(A \cap B) \to Cn(A) ⊕ Cn(B)\) given by \(φ̃n := (iAB n♯,-iBA n♯) and ψ̃n: Cn(A) ⊕ Cn(B) \to Cn(A+B)\) given by \(ψ̃n := jA n♯ + jB n♯\text{,}\) is exact.
Theorem 7.2.35. Small Chains Theorem.
Let \(X\) be a topological space with subspaces \(A,B\) such that \(X = Int(A) \cup Int(B)\text{.}\) Then \(H_n(X) \cong H_n(A+B)\) for all \(n\text{.}\)
