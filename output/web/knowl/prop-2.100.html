Proposition 2.2.31.

Let X be a topological space. (a) If \sim is an equivalence relation on X, then the quotient topology on X/\sim induced by \sim equals the quotient topology on X/\sim induced by the equivalence map q:X \to X/\sim (defined by q(p) := [p] for all p in X). (b) If f:X \to Y is a surjection, then the quotient space (Y,\cT f) is homeomorphic to the quotient space (X/\sim,\cT \sim ) induced by the equivalence relation on X defined by x \sim x' if and only if f(x) = f(x') for all x,x' \in X; moreover, the homeomorphism g:X/\sim \to Y satisfies g \circ q = f, where q is the equivalence map.
