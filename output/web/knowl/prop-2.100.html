Proposition 2.2.35.

Let \(X\) be a topological space.
  1. If \(\sim\) is an equivalence relation on \(X\text{,}\) then the quotient topology on \(X/\sim\) induced by \(\sim\) equals the quotient topology on \(X/\sim\) induced by the equivalence map \(q:X \to X/\sim\) (defined by \(q(p) := [p]\) for all \(p \in X\)).
  2. If \(f:X \to Y\) is a surjection, then the quotient space \((Y,\cT f)\) is homeomorphic to the quotient space \((X/\sim,\cT \sim )\) induced by the equivalence relation on \(X\) defined by \(x \sim x'\) if and only if \(f(x) = f(x')\) for all \(x,x' \in X\text{;}\) moreover, the homeomorphism \(g:X/\sim \to Y\) satisfies \(g \circ q = f\text{,}\) where \(q\) is the equivalence map.
