Theorem 2.3.10.

Let X be a topological space and let A and B be subsets of X. (1) ClX(A) is closed in X. (1') IntX(A) is open in X. (2) If A \sse B \sse X, then ClX(A) \sse ClX(B) and IntX(A) \sse IntX(B). (3) A is closed in X iff A = ClX(A). (3') B is open in X iff B = IntX(B). (4) ClX(A) is the smallest closed set in X containing A. (4') IntX(A) is the largest open set in X contained in A.
