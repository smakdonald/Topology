Theorem 2.3.10.

Let \(X\) be a topological space and let \(A\) and \(B\) be subsets of \(X\text{.}\)
  1. \(\text{Cl}_X(A)\) is closed in \(X\) and \(IntX(A)\) is open in \(X\text{.}\)
  2. If A \sse B \sse X, then \text{Cl}_X(A) \sse \text{Cl}_X(B) and IntX(A) \sse IntX(B).
  3. \(A\) is closed in \(X\) iff \(A = \text{Cl}_X(A)\) and \(B\) is open in \(X\) iff \(B = IntX(B)\text{.}\)
  4. \(\text{Cl}_X(A)\) is the smallest closed set in \(X\) containing \(A\text{,}\) and \(IntX(A)\) is the largest open set in \(X\) contained in \(A\text{.}\)
