Definition 2.1.1.

A topology on a set \(X\) is a collection \(𝒯\) of subsets of \(X\) satisfying:
  1. \(∅\) and \(X\) are elements of \(𝒯\text{;}\)
  2. whenever \(A_α\) is in \(𝒯\) for all \(α\text{,}\) then \(∪_α A_α\) is in \(𝒯\text{;}\) and
  3. whenever \(A_1,...,A_n\) are in \(𝒯\text{,}\) then \(∩_{i=1}^n A_i\) is in \(𝒯\text{.}\)
