Definition 4.3.1.

Let X and Y be topological spaces and let g,h: X → Y continuous functions. A homotopy from g to h is a family of maps ft: X → Y for all t ∈ I such that
  1. f0 = g,
  2. f1 = h, and
  3. the function H: X × I → Y defined by H(p,t) := ft(p), for all p ∈ X and t ∈ I, is continuous.
The function H is also called a homotopy from g to h. The function g is said to be homotopic to h, written g ≃ h.
