Definition 3.2.2.

Let d be a metric on a set X. For any p ∈ X and r ∈ ℝ with r > 0, the ball of radius r centered at p is Bd(p,r) := {q ∈ X | d(p,q) < r}. The metric basis on X induced by d is the collection ℬd := {Bd(p,r) | p ∈ X and r > 0}. The metric topology on X induced by d is the topology 𝒯d := 𝒯(ℬd) generated by the metric basis.
