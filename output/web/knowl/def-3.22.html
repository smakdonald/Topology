Definition 3.2.2.

Let d be a metric on a set X. For any p \in X and r \in \R with r > 0, the ball of radius r centered at p is Bd(p,r) := {q \in X | d(p,q) < r}. The metric basis on X induced by d is the collection \cB d := {Bd(p,r) | p \in X and r > 0}. The metric topology on X induced by d is the topology \cT d := \cT (\cB d) generated by the metric basis.
