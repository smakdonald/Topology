There are 3 views of G \times H: Using presentations (if G = \langle A | R \rangle and H = \langle B | S \rangle, then G \times H = \langle A \cup B | R \cup S \cup {ab = ba | a \in A, b \in B} >), as a Cartesian product set with componentwise multiplication, and as the largest group generated by G and H such that the subgroups G and H commute.
