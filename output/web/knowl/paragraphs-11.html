Compactness and Euclidean topology:.

Theorem 3.4.17.

If a,b \in \R with a < b, then the interval [a,b], with the subspace topology from (\R,\cT_{\text{Eucl}}), is compact.

Definition 3.4.18.

Let (X,d) be a metric space. The diameter of a nonempty subset Y of X is diam(Y) := sup{d(p,q) | p,q \in Y}. The set Y is bounded if diam(Y) is finite.

Theorem 3.4.19. Heine-Borel Theorem.

Let Y be a subspace of (\R^n,\cT_{\text{Eucl}}). Then Y is compact iff Y is a closed and bounded subset of \R^n.
