Let \(X\) be a topological space. Then
  1. \(\es\) and \(X\) are closed in \(X\text{;}\)
  2. whenever \(A_\alpha\) is closed in \(X\) for all \(\alpha\text{,}\) then \(\cap_\alpha A_\alpha\) is closed in \(X\text{;}\) and
  3. whenever \(A_1,\dots,A_n\) are closed in \(X\text{,}\) then \(\cup_{i=1n}A_i\) is closed in \(X\text{.}\)
