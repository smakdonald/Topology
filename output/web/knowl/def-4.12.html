Definition 4.2.2.

Let Y be a subspace of a topological space X. A deformation retraction of X onto Y is a family of maps ft: X \to X for all t \in I such that:
  1. f0 = 1X,
  2. f1(X) = Y,
  3. ft|Y = 1Y for all t \in I, and
  4. the function H: X \times I \to X defined by H(p,t) := ft(p), for all p \in X and t \in I, is continuous.
The function H is also called a deformation retraction from X to Y. The space Y is called a deformation retract of X, written X ‚áù Y.
