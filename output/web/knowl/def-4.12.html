Definition 4.2.2.

Let \(Y\) be a subspace of a topological space \(X\text{.}\) A deformation retraction of \(X\) onto \(Y\) is a family of maps \(f_t: X \to X\) for all \(t \in I\) such that:
  1. \(f_0 = 1_X\text{,}\)
  2. \(f_1(X) = Y\text{,}\)
  3. \(f_t|_Y = 1_Y\) for all \(t \in I\text{,}\) and
  4. the function \(H: X \times I \to X\) defined by \(H(p,t) := ft(p)\text{,}\) for all \(p \in X\) and \(t \in I\text{,}\) is continuous.
The function \(H\) is also called a deformation retraction from \(X\) to \(Y\text{.}\) The space \(Y\) is called a deformation retract of \(X\text{,}\) written \(X ‚áù Y\text{.}\)
