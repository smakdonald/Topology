Proposition 3.4.20.

  1. If \(\sim\) is the smallest equivalence relation on \(I\) such that \(0 \sim 1\text{,}\) then \(I/\sim \cong S^1\text{,}\) with a homeomorphism that maps the equivalence class \([s]\) to the point \(( \cos(2 \pi s), \sin(2 \pi s) )\) for all \(s \in I\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\sim\) is the smallest equivalence relation on \(I \times I\) such that \((s,1) \sim (1,1)\) for all \(s \in I\) and \((0,t) \sim (1,1) \sim (1,t)\) for all \(t \in I\text{,}\) then \((I \times I)/\sim \cong D^2\text{,}\) with a homeomorphism that maps the equivalence class \([(1,1)]\) to the point \((1,0)\) and maps \([(s,0)]\) to \((\cos(2\pi s),\sin(2\pi s))\) for all \(s \in I\text{.}\)
