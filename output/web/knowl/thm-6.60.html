Theorem 6.2.9.

Let X be a PC, LPC, SLSC space and x0 ∈ X. Then:
  1. (SC Covering Space Thm): There is a SC covering space p:(X̃,x̃0) → (X,x0), and there is covering space group action of G := π1(X,x0) on X̃ that induces p; hence (X̃/G,[x̃0]) ≅ (X,x0). Moreover, if X is a CW complex, then X̃ is a CW complex, and for each open n-cell σ of X there are |G| open n-cells of X̃ mapping via p to σ such that the action of each g ∈ G on X̃ permutes these n-cells.
  2. (Covering Space Existence Thm): For each subgroup H < π1(X,x0), there is a covering space rH: (X̃/H,[x̃0]H) → (X,x0) such that rH ∘ qH = p, where qH: X̃ → X̃/H is the quotient map, and H = rH∗(π1(X̃/H,[x̃0]H)).
