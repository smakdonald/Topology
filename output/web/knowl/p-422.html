Let X and Y be topological spaces and let g,h: X \to Y continuous functions. A homotopy from g to h is a family of maps ft: X \to Y for all t \in I such that
  1. f0 = g,
  2. f1 = h, and
  3. the function H: X \times I \to Y defined by H(p,t) := ft(p), for all p \in X and t \in I, is continuous.
The function H is also called a homotopy from g to h. The function g is said to be homotopic to h, written g \simeq h.
