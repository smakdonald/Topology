Let (X,x0) be a PC, LPC, SLSC space, let G := π1(X,x0), and let p: (X̃,x̃0) → (X,x0) be the SC covering. Let r: (Z,z0) → (X,x0) be any PC covering space. Then:
  1. Galois Correspondence Thm.
    The maps {subgroups H of G} ↔ {isomorphism classes of PC pointed coverings of (X,x0)} defined by H → (rH: (X̃/H,[x̃0]H) → (X,x0)) and (t: (Y,y0) → (X,x0)) → Im t∗ = t∗(π1(Y,y0)) are inverse bijections.
  2. Universal Covering Thm.
    There is a covering space map q: X̃ → Z such that r ∘ q = p.
  3. Action Thm.
    Let H := r∗(π1(Z,z0)) and let NG(H) := the normalizer of H in G := { g ∈ G | gHg-1 ⊆ H }. The group of deck transformations of r: Z → X is isomorphic to NG(H)/H and has a covering space action on Z. Moreover, (H is normal in G) iff (G/H has a covering space action on Z inducing r) iff (the covering space r:Z → X is normal).
