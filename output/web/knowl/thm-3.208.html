Theorem 3.5.19. Urysohn's Lemma.

Let \(X\) be a \(T_1\) space. The following are equivalent:
  1. \(X\) is \(T_4\text{.}\)
  2. Whenever \(A\) and \(B\) are disjoint closed subsets of \(X\text{,}\) there is a continuous function \(f : X \to [0,1]\) such that \(f(A) =\{0\}\) and \(f(B) =\{1\}\text{.}\)
