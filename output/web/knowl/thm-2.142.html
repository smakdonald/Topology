Theorem 2.2.52.

Let \(X\) be a topological space, let \(\sim\) be an equivalence relation on \(X\text{,}\) and let \(q:X \to X/\sim\) be the quotient map. Let \(A\) be a subspace of \(X\) and let \(q(A)\) have the subspace topology in \(X/\sim\text{.}\)
  1. The relation \(\approx\) on \(A\) defined by \([a \approx a'\) if and only if \(a \sim a'\) for all \(a,a' \in A]\) is an equivalence relation on \(A\text{.}\)
  2. If \(A = q\inv(q(A))\) and \(A\) is either an open or closed subset of \(X\text{,}\) then the the function \(q|Aq(A):A \to q(A)\) is a quotient map and the function \(h: A/\approx \to q(A)\) defined by \(h([a]) := [h(a)]\) for all\([a] \in A/\approx\) is a homeomorphism.
